Summer Palace

The Summer Palace, formerly known as the Qing Ripple Garden, was built in the 15th year of the Qianlong era of the Qing Dynasty and is located in the western suburbs of Beijing. It is a large landscape garden based on Kunming Lake and Wanshou Mountain, modeled on the West Lake in Hangzhou, and built by drawing on the design techniques of the gardens in Jiangnan. The Summer Palace in Beijing is soaked with the blood and sweat of the Chinese people, embodies the wisdom and creativity of the Chinese people, and shows the essence of Chinese gardening art.

The Forbidden City

The Forbidden City in Beijing is the royal palace of China's Ming and Qing dynasties, formerly known as the Forbidden City, and the Palace of Versailles in France, Buckingham Palace in the United Kingdom, the White House in the United States, and the Kremlin in Russia, known as the five largest palaces in the world. Beijing Palace Museum was founded on October 10, 1925, is a Chinese comprehensive museum, is China's largest museum of ancient culture and art, is the first national patriotic education demonstration base. The collection of the Beijing Palace Museum includes, but is not limited to, the imperial palaces of the Ming and Qing dynasties and their collections, covering almost the entire history of the development of ancient Chinese civilization and almost all categories of cultural relics.
Badaling-Mutianyu Great Wall

Badaling-Mutianyu Great Wall tourist area includes two major parts of Badaling Great Wall scenic spot and Mutianyu Great Wall scenic spot. The Badaling Great Wall is the first of the Great Wall of China, the essence of the Ming Great Wall, with its dangerous terrain and majestic construction, and has been a military strategic location for "guarding the capital" since ancient times. It is the longest Great Wall in China and one of the 16 famous scenic spots in Beijing.
