Hangzhou West Lake

Hangzhou West Lake is famous in China and abroad for its beautiful lake and mountains and many famous monuments, and is a famous tourist destination in China, also known as heaven on earth. The hills in the scenic area are not more than 400 meters high, and are ringed in the south, west and north of the West Lake, among which the Wu Mountain and the Jewel Mountain are like two arms, one to the south and one to the north, stretching towards the city, forming a beautiful spatial outline of Hangzhou. The total area of the scenic spot is 49 square kilometers, including 6.5 square kilometers of the lake, with the lake as the main body, which was formerly called Wulin Water, Qiantang Lake, Xizi Lake, and was called West Lake in the Song Dynasty; there are "Ten Views of West Lake", "Ten Views of New West Lake" and "Three Reviews of Ten Views of West Lake". said.

Lingyin Temple

Lingyin Temple, also known as Yunlin Temple, is located in Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province, backed by the North Peak and facing the Feilai Peak. It was built in the first year of Xianhe of the Eastern Jin Dynasty (326) and covers an area of about 87,000 square meters. The founder of Lingyin Temple was the West Indian monk Hui Li, who was given a field and expanded by Emperor Wu of the Southern Dynasty. The temple is mainly composed of Tianwang Hall, Daxiongbao Hall, Yakushi Hall, Dharma Hall and Huayan Hall as the central axis, with 500 Lohan Halls, Jigong Hall, Huayan Pavilion, Daibei Building and Abbot's Building on both sides, and the current abbot is Venerable Guangquan.

Suzhou classical gardens

Suzhou classical gardens, also known as "Suzhou gardens", originate from the Spring and Autumn Period and are one of the representative schools of classical Chinese architecture. The architectural style of gardens represented by the Humble Administrator's Garden, the Garden of the Staying Place, the Net Master's Garden and the Huanxiu Villa has influenced the architectural style of the entire southern cities. Suzhou gardens are "literati's landscape gardens" with profound cultural significance, using unique gardening techniques to combine pavilions, terraces, buildings, pavilions, springs, rocks, flowers and trees to create a harmonious living environment between humans and nature in the city, which constitutes the overall characteristics of Suzhou classical gardens.
